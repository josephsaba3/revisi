# SplitPea Brand Voice Guide

## Who we're talking to

Small business owners, freelancers, and small agencies. People who are good at what they do but don't have a growth team or a testing budget. They're practical. They want tools that work and don't waste their time. Some of them have never run an A/B test. Some tried Google Optimize before it shut down and haven't found anything affordable since.

They're not dumb. They just have other things to worry about.

## Who we are (as a voice)

The competent friend who happens to know a lot about this stuff. We talk to people like equals. We don't sell, we don't explain things like we're talking to a child, and we don't try to impress anyone. We've done this before and we're happy to walk you through it.

We're calm, we're clear, and we're helpful. That's the whole thing.

## Core voice traits

**Clear over clever.** Say what you mean. If a normal person wouldn't say it in a conversation, don't write it. No jargon for the sake of jargon. "See which version gets more clicks" is better than "optimize your conversion funnel."

**Confident, not loud.** We know what we built and we're not shy about it. But we also don't need to tear anyone else down or hype ourselves up. We just say what the thing does.

**Warm, not quirky.** Friendly and easy to talk to, but we're not doing a comedy bit. No mascot voice, no forced humor. We're a real tool for real work.

**Encouraging, not patronizing.** If someone's running their first experiment, we want them to feel like they can do it. But we don't hold their hand so tight it gets weird. We give them what they need and get out of the way.

**Small by choice.** We're not pretending to be enterprise. We're not "scaling to serve teams of thousands." We built this for people like us.

## Writing rules (what makes it sound human)

These are based on what actually separates human writing from AI-generated copy. Follow them and the writing will sound like a person wrote it. Break them and it'll sound like you pasted something from ChatGPT.

### Vary the rhythm

AI writes sentences that are all roughly the same length. Same cadence, same structure, paragraph after paragraph. Humans don't do that.

Mix it up. Some sentences should be short. Others can run a bit longer, maybe with a parenthetical or a comma that lets the reader breathe. Let the content dictate the pace, not a formula.

Bad: "SplitPea is a simple tool. It helps you test your website. It shows you what works. It's easy to use."

Better: "SplitPea helps you test what's working on your site. Set up an experiment in a few minutes, split your traffic, and get a clear answer. No guesswork."

### Be specific, not sweeping

AI loves to zoom out and connect everything to some grand theme. "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape..." Nobody talks like that. Get to the point and stay close to the actual thing you're describing.

Bad: "In today's competitive business environment, data-driven decisions are more important than ever."

Better: "You probably have a hunch about which headline works better. SplitPea tells you if you're right."

### Take a position

AI hedges. It presents both sides, qualifies everything, and ends up saying nothing. Humans have opinions. SplitPea has opinions.

We think A/B testing should be affordable. We think most tools in this space are overpriced for what small businesses actually need. We think you shouldn't need a statistics degree to read your own results. Say it.

### Use simple verbs

AI avoids "is" and "has" like they're boring. It replaces them with "serves as," "features," "offers," "represents." These sound like a press release.

Bad: "SplitPea features an intuitive dashboard that offers real-time insights."

Better: "SplitPea has a dashboard. It shows your results as they come in."

### Don't summarize what you just said

If you catch yourself writing "Overall," or "In summary," or restating a point you made two sentences ago, delete it. The reader was there. They just read it.

### Skip the negative parallelism

"It's not just a tool, it's a partner." "It's not about data, it's about decisions." This construction is everywhere in AI copy and it almost always sounds hollow. Just say the positive thing directly.

Bad: "It's not just A/B testing. It's a smarter way to grow."

Better: "Run a test. See what works. Do more of that."

### Avoid the dead words

These words have been used so heavily by AI that they've lost all meaning. Don't use them in SplitPea copy:

delve, intricate, tapestry, pivotal, underscore, landscape, foster, testament, enhance, crucial, leverage, elevate, empower, robust, seamless, cutting-edge, harness, streamline, spearhead, holistic, synergy, paradigm, revolutionize

Also avoid: "game-changer," "next-level," "supercharge," "unlock the power of," and anything that sounds like it belongs on a LinkedIn carousel.

### Don't stack threes

AI defaults to listing things in groups of three, especially adjectives. "Innovative, powerful, and intuitive." "Fast, flexible, and reliable." It's a tell. Sometimes two is fine. Sometimes four. Let it be uneven.

### Go easy on the em dashes

One per page is fine. Two is pushing it. If you're using them to punch up every other sentence, you're doing the thing. Use commas or parentheses instead. They're perfectly good punctuation.

### No formatting as a crutch

Don't bold random words for emphasis. Don't use bullet points where a sentence would do. Don't put emojis in headers. If the writing needs heavy formatting to make sense, the writing probably needs a rewrite.

### Let some roughness in

Perfect, polished prose with zero friction reads as machine-generated now. A slightly conversational register, a sentence fragment here and there, an aside that adds personality (not forced personality, real personality) — that's what makes writing feel like a person sat down and thought about it.

Not sloppy. Just human.

## What we sound like (in practice)

### Onboarding

*Not this:* "Welcome to SplitPea! You're about to embark on a journey toward data-driven growth. Let's get started on your first experiment!"

*This:* "You're in. Let's set up your first test."

### Explaining a feature

*Not this:* "SplitPea leverages a sophisticated Bayesian statistical engine to deliver actionable, real-time insights that empower you to make data-driven decisions with confidence."

*This:* "SplitPea watches your experiment and tells you when there's a winner. You don't have to do the math."

### Error message

*Not this:* "Oopsie! Something went sideways 🙈 Don't worry, our team is on it!"

*This:* "Something went wrong loading your experiment. Try refreshing. If it keeps happening, let us know."

### Empty state (no experiments yet)

*Not this:* "Your dashboard is empty! Start your optimization journey today and discover what resonates with your audience!"

*This:* "No experiments yet. Want to set one up?"

### Pricing page

*Not this:* "Unlock the full potential of your website with our flexible, value-driven pricing tiers designed to scale with your business."

*This:* "Pick a plan. They're all pretty affordable."

### Feature description (visual editor)

*Not this:* "Our intuitive visual editor empowers you to create sophisticated test variations without writing a single line of code, seamlessly integrating with your existing workflow."

*This:* "Click the thing you want to change. Type the new version. That's the whole process."

### CTA (call to action)

*Not this:* "Start Your Free Trial and Unlock the Power of Data-Driven Optimization!"

*This:* "Try it free."

### Results page

*Not this:* "Congratulations! Your experiment has yielded statistically significant results, demonstrating a clear winner that will enhance your conversion strategy."

*This:* "Version B is the winner. 94% confidence. You can end this test and go with B."

### Blog post intro

*Not this:* "In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, small businesses must leverage every available tool to stay competitive. A/B testing has emerged as a crucial strategy for optimizing website performance."

*This:* "Most small business websites are leaving money on the table. Not because the product is bad or the copy is terrible, but because nobody's tested whether a different headline or a bigger button would work better. That's a solvable problem."

### About page

*Not this:* "SplitPea was born from a passion for democratizing data-driven decision making. We believe every business, regardless of size, deserves access to powerful optimization tools."

*This:* "When Google Optimize shut down, the cheapest alternatives started around $200 a month. That's a lot if you're a freelancer or a small shop just trying to figure out if your homepage is working. We built SplitPea because that gap seemed wrong."

## Tone by context

The voice stays the same everywhere. The tone shifts a little depending on where someone is.

**Marketing and landing pages:** Warmest and most conversational. This is where we connect with people and show we understand the problem. Still direct, still honest, but we can take our time a bit.

**In-app UI and microcopy:** Short and functional. We're friendly, but we don't waste words. The person's trying to do something and we should help them do it, not chat at them.

**Help docs and support:** Patient. More structured. Still plain language, still us, but we explain things step by step because that's what someone needs when they're stuck.

**Error states and edge cases:** Calm and matter-of-fact. No drama, no jokes. Say what happened and what they can try. That's it.

**Email and lifecycle:** Conversational and brief. Write like you're sending a quick note to someone you've worked with before. Don't over-explain. Don't oversell.

## Quick-reference checklist

Before publishing any SplitPea copy, read it out loud and ask:

1. Would a person actually say this? Like, out loud, to another person?
2. Does every sentence sound roughly the same length and structure? (If yes, fix it.)
3. Are there any words from the dead words list?
4. Did I use "It's not X, it's Y" anywhere? (Cut it.)
5. Did I summarize or restate something I said two sentences ago? (Cut it.)
6. Is there a group of exactly three adjectives or benefits? (Make it two, or four, or just rewrite it.)
7. Does it sound like it's trying too hard to sound casual? (Dial it back. Real casual doesn't try.)
8. Could I swap in any other product name and this copy would still work? (If yes, it's too generic. Make it about SplitPea specifically.)
9. Am I using bold, bullets, or headers because the writing doesn't stand on its own? (Rewrite instead.)
10. Does it feel like a real person with opinions wrote this, or like a machine produced something safe and inoffensive? (You know which one we want.)
