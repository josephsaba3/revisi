# Rewrite Rules

Use these rules for suggested rewrites, before/after examples, CTAs, and generated mini voice guides.

1. Keep the user's intent.
   - Preserve the actual offer, promise, audience, and constraints.
   - Do not reposition the business unless the report explicitly recommends repositioning.

2. Do not over-polish.
   - Make the line clearer and more believable, not smoother for its own sake.
   - Prefer a sentence a real founder or team member could say.

3. Prefer specific nouns and verbs.
   - Replace `solutions`, `experiences`, and `workflows` with the thing being sold or the task being done.
   - Replace `empower`, `leverage`, and `optimize` with concrete action verbs.

4. Cut filler.
   - Remove throat-clearing, generic transitions, duplicated claims, and inflated modifiers.

5. Replace abstract claims with concrete outcomes.
   - Weak: `Save time and streamline your workflow.`
   - Better: `Turn a messy feedback spreadsheet into a prioritized test plan in under an hour.`

6. Keep the brand's level of warmth.
   - If the source is plain and practical, do not make it playful.
   - If the source is warm and founder-led, do not make it enterprise-formal.

7. Avoid making small brands sound enterprise.
   - Do not add `enterprise-grade`, `mission-critical`, or `at scale` unless the source supports that market.

8. Avoid fake proof.
   - Do not invent metrics, customer names, testimonials, awards, certifications, guarantees, or screenshots.
   - Use placeholders only when clearly labeled, such as `[customer proof needed]`.

9. Preserve supported claims.
   - If the source provides verified size, speed, accuracy, pricing, or customer claims, keep them intact unless the user asks otherwise.

10. Match CTA to intent.
   - Early-stage or high-consideration: use `Book a short consult`, `See a sample audit`, or `Send your page for review`.
   - Product-led or low-friction: use `Try the demo`, `Create your first test`, or `Upload your copy`.
   - Avoid standalone `Learn more` unless the next page is genuinely educational.
