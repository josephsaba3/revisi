# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Revisi serves marketers, founders, and agencies who need to understand whether a website sounds like the brand it is meant to represent. They may be auditing one page before launch, reviewing a growing marketing site, or checking a client site across many pages. Their shared job is to find tone drift, unclear claims, generic language, weak proof, and AI-sounding copy quickly enough to revise it.

The default register is product because the durable surface is the audit workspace and report. The marketing site can use brand-register moves when needed, but future product work should serve the task: scan, diagnose, prioritize, revise, and rescore.

## Product Purpose

Revisi reads website copy like a focused copy chief. It extracts visible page copy, scores the page against a brand voice profile, flags line-level issues, and gives practical rewrites. Success means a user can see what is off, understand why it matters, and know exactly which lines to fix next without wading through a bloated writing platform.

The product should feel useful whether the user has a small one-page site or a larger content estate. It should stay niche, sharp, and task-shaped rather than expanding into a broad Grammarly-style writing suite.

## Brand Personality

Revisi should feel editorial, precise, useful, and quietly confident. It can be witty in small moments, but it should not become cute or toy-like. The voice should respect the user's craft: direct enough to be useful, specific enough to earn trust, and restrained enough that the report feels like a professional review rather than a generated critique.

The emotional goal is relief: the user should feel that the messy question of "does this sound right?" has been made concrete, inspectable, and fixable.

## Anti-references

Revisi should not look or feel like:

- Generic AI SaaS with interchangeable gradients, vague productivity language, and oversized empty polish.
- A Grammarly clone or broad writing assistant.
- A busy, dense SEO or enterprise analytics tool.
- A playful copywriting toy.
- An enterprise dashboard with heavy navigation, bloated modules, or generic admin patterns.
- A product that implies every site needs a giant platform to improve its copy.

## Design Principles

1. Make voice visible. Treat copy quality as something the user can inspect sentence by sentence, not as a vague score.
2. Stay niche and sharp. Add only the controls and surfaces that help users audit, revise, and rescore website copy.
3. Editorial confidence over AI gloss. The interface should feel like a working copy desk: precise, opinionated, and calm.
4. Useful at any site size. The same core workflow should make sense for one page, a growing site, or an agency client account.
5. Diagnose before prescribing. Show what triggered the flag and why before asking the user to accept a rewrite.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard-first report review, clear focus states, reduced-motion preferences, and color-blind-safe status cues that do not rely on color alone. Keep line-level findings readable at normal zoom, preserve sufficient contrast in pink, green, muted, and dark sections, and avoid motion that distracts from reading.
