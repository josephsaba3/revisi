# Product

## Register

product

## Users

SplitPea is for founders, freelancers, agencies, marketers, and small teams who run modern websites and want to test changes without adopting a heavy analytics or enterprise experimentation stack. They are usually close to the work: changing landing pages, pricing pages, lead forms, campaign pages, client sites, and product copy themselves or with a small team.

They need practical confidence. They want to know whether a change helped, what to do next, and how to keep a record of what they learned.

## Product Purpose

SplitPea helps people create, run, measure, and decide website experiments with a lightweight snippet and a focused workflow. It exists to make A/B testing feel approachable, trustworthy, and useful for smaller teams that do not have dedicated growth operations.

Success means a user can install once, set up an experiment quickly, understand the result without statistical hand-holding, and make a calm decision based on evidence.

## Brand Personality

Confident but not arrogant, clear, helpful.

The product should feel calm, warm, and capable. It should explain experimentation in plain language, respect the user's time, and make good testing behavior feel achievable. It can have personality, but not quirk for its own sake.

## Anti-references

SplitPea should not look or sound like enterprise experimentation software, enterprise analytics dashboards, or a tool built only for teams with dedicated growth departments.

It should also avoid startup cliches: inflated claims, noisy hype, quirky mascots, forced playfulness, over-polished VC SaaS language, and anything that makes the product feel arrogant. The tone should be confident, not self-important; warm, not cute.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with practical clarity: show users what they can do, what changed, and what decision is available next.
2. Earn confidence through evidence: make metrics, confidence, and outcomes easy to understand without overselling certainty.
3. Keep the workflow narrow: avoid feature sprawl and design patterns that make testing feel bigger than it is.
4. Feel calm under ambiguity: support inconclusive results, low traffic, and waiting without making the user feel lost.
5. Be warm without becoming quirky: use human copy and approachable visuals while keeping the product serious enough to trust.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for accessible, readable interfaces that work for non-specialists and busy users. Maintain strong contrast, clear focus states, keyboard-friendly navigation, reduced-motion compatibility for decorative motion, and copy that does not rely on statistical jargon.
